    FAQs for NFT

    What purpose do NFTs serve in games?

    NFTs are characterized by their uniqueness, whereby they allow gamers to buy and sell or resell gaming assets. NFTs allow exclusive gameplay through their unique gaming assets that have no duplicates and cannot be copied. As an example, when you reach a certain level during an NFT game, you get unique tokens that have real value. The same will be true for purchases you make during a game.

    Can you make money playing NFT games?

    NFT games are decentralized, and it offers users an opportunity to utilize game assets outside the game as NFTs. And the users can make money selling those NFTs in marketplaces. In other words, what you gain during a game, will be assets of real value. You will be able to sell them for a price, and those particular assets will not be duplicates available anywhere.

    What do your NFT gaming development services comprise?

    Our services include real-world assets, asset tokenization, tokenized identification cards, etc. These assets can take the shape of characters, trading cards, weapons, gaming accessories, vehicles, and much more.

    What is a non-fungible token?

    Commonly, you have fungible tokens in games, which means they can be exchanged with each other because they have the same value. NFTs or non-fungible tokens cant be used the same way i.e., they cant be exchanged since they have unique identification and cant be replicated.

    Why should we implement NFTs in games?

    NFTs for games let you deliver a realistic gameplay experience where users can buy and sell virtual assets. NFT platforms allow a realistic gaming experience, wherein you offer rewards.

    Ethereum

    Ethereum is among the most popular platforms for NFT development because its transaction history as well as token metadata, can be publicly verified. Proving ownership is easier, and NFTs are portable across products.

    Flow

    Flow, a decentralized blockchain, is built for a new generation of digital assets, apps, and games. Widely used for building the NFT marketplace and NFTs by using the Cadence programming language, it is secure and stable.

    Tezos

    Tezos, an open-source blockchain platform, is designed to meet the needs of apps and assets bolstered through a community of builders, researchers, and validators. The platform uses a pre-compiled FA2 NFT contract written in LIGO smart contract language.

    Wax

    Worldwide Asset exchange (aka WAX) is considered the King of NFTs since it's a leading blockchain-based on user aggregate and transactions. WAX has brought NFTs as a mass-market exchange, promising to be the safest to use.

    Cardano

    Cardano, a blockchain platform comprising technologies and tools for heightened security and sustainability in decentralized systems and applications, also catering to NFTs and other digital assets development.

    IPFS

    IPFS, a peer-to-peer hypermedia protocol, is built for decentralized media storage in the absence of NFT media file direct storage on a blockchain. IPFS is built ensure that all relevant data is stored securely.

    Filecoin

    Filecoin offers a decentralized storage network specifically built for storing critical information. It includes all that a developer needs for decentralized storage for NFTs and is considered a robust solution for storage.

    Pinata

    Pinata is a renowned platform where you can upload and manage files on IPFS. It offers a secure and verifiable system within which you can store and manage the files you need for NFTs in game development.

    React

    React, a front-end JavaScript library creates user interfaces or UI components. You can leverage React's strong and versatile platform to develop useful and engaging front-end elements that reinforce your web application.

    Angular
